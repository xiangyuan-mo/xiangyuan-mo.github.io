About Me

I am a first-year Master's student in Economics at Duke University (expected May 2027), where I maintain a 4.00 GPA. I currently serve as a Research Assistant for Professor Alon Brav at Duke Fuqua, working on empirical corporate finance and institutional investor behavior.

My research interests lie at the intersection of empirical corporate finance, causal inference, and machine learning. I am particularly interested in institutional investor voting behavior, credit rating determinants, and the application of modern econometric methods to financial markets.

Prior to Duke, I completed my B.A. in Quantitative Economics at Renmin University of China, where I was awarded the National Second Prize in the Chinese Mathematics Competition and the Best Modeling Award at the RUC Fintech Hackathon. I also held quantitative analyst internships at CITIC Securities and Sinolink Securities.
