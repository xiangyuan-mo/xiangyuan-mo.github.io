Working Papers

Government Affiliation and Rating Inequality: Uncovering the Corporate Credit Rating Black Box
with Lei Ge (Renmin University of China)  ·  Working Paper, 2025
We study whether government affiliation confers a systematic credit rating premium using a large panel of ~132,000 firm-quarter observations from WRDS (2000–2025). Our identification strategy combines baseline OLS with firm and time fixed effects, shock-interacted difference-in-differences exploiting the 2008 GFC and COVID-19 as exogenous shocks, a Triple-DID design using pre-shock credit standing as a third dimension, and Double/Debiased Machine Learning with Causal Forest for heterogeneous treatment effects. We find a causal rating premium of approximately 0.38 notches for government-affiliated firms. The effect is amplified post-2008 and reverses for speculative-grade firms during COVID-19, suggesting that affiliation serves as a credibility signal in normal times but a liability signal during systemic stress.

Work in Progress

Institutional Investor Voting Behavior: Evidence from SEC 13F and N-PX Filings
Research Assistant to Prof. Alon Brav (Duke Fuqua)  ·  In progress, 2025–present
This project constructs a comprehensive dataset linking institutional investors' portfolio holdings (SEC 13F) with their proxy voting records (SEC N-PX) to analyze the determinants and consequences of institutional voting behavior. The data pipeline integrates Python- and Stata-based matching algorithms across multiple WRDS databases.

Nepal Earthquake Damage Grade Prediction
Duke STA-521 (Statistical Learning)  ·  December 2024  ·  Best Prediction in Class
Built a Geography AutoEncoder compressing 12,568 location categories into 16 learned embedding vectors. Combined with XGBoost and Random Forest ensembles and Optuna hyperparameter tuning, achieved a Quadratic Weighted Kappa of ~0.618 — the highest performance in the class.

RUC Fintech Hackathon: Housing Price Prediction
RUC Quantitative Modeling Competition  ·  2024  ·  Best Modeling Award
Preprocessed raw housing data with Pandas; filled missing values with KNN; used OpenFE for automated deep feature engineering. Stacked OLS, Lasso, Ridge, XGBoost, Random Forest, and Wide Neural Networks (inspired by Cheng et al. 2016); used Optuna for hyperparameter search. Reduced MAE substantially by mixing base models and constructing counterfactual data to mitigate class imbalance.

Market Sentiment and Implied Volatility Forecasting
RUC Research Fund  ·  March 2023 – April 2024
Processed 630,000 text observations from online trader communities to construct investor sentiment indicators using BERT. Predicted implied volatility and set up LSTM models to capture the dynamic relationship between market sentiment and options pricing.
