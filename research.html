Working Papers
Government Affiliation and Rating Inequality: Uncovering the Corporate Credit Rating Black Box
with Lei Ge (Renmin University of China)  ·  Working Paper, 2025
We study whether government affiliation confers a systematic credit rating premium using ~132,000 firm-quarter observations from WRDS (2000–2025). Our identification strategy combines baseline OLS with firm and time fixed effects, shock-interacted difference-in-differences exploiting the 2008 GFC and COVID-19 as exogenous shocks, a Triple-DID using pre-shock credit standing as a third dimension, and Double/Debiased Machine Learning with Causal Forest for heterogeneous treatment effects. We find a causal rating premium of approximately 0.38 notches for government-affiliated firms. The effect is amplified post-2008 and reverses for speculative-grade firms during COVID-19.

Work in Progress
Institutional Investor Voting Behavior: Evidence from SEC 13F and N-PX Filings
Research Assistant to Prof. Alon Brav (Duke Fuqua)  ·  In progress, 2025–present
This project constructs a comprehensive dataset linking institutional investors' portfolio holdings (SEC 13F) with their proxy voting records (SEC N-PX) to analyze the determinants and consequences of institutional voting behavior. The data pipeline integrates Python- and Stata-based matching algorithms across multiple WRDS databases.
